Did Stage Racing Increase Chaos in NASCAR?

Cup Series · 2014–2019 analysis

In 2017, NASCAR introduced stage racing. Critics argued that adding guaranteed restarts would increase late-race aggression and crashes.

I built a race-level dataset of all NASCAR Cup Series races from 2014–2019 to test a simple question:

Did stage racing increase unscheduled cautions?

Data

I manually constructed a structured dataset of 216 Cup races (2014–2019). Each race includes:

  • Total cautions
  • Scheduled cautions (stage ends, competition cautions)
  • Unscheduled cautions (loss of control, crashes, debris)
  • Track classification (short, intermediate, road course, superspeedway, non-standard)

The core outcome variable is unscheduled cautions per race.

Descriptive Results

Unscheduled cautions declined steadily over the sample period:

  • 2014: 8.06
  • 2015: 7.86
  • 2016: 6.97
  • 2017: 5.97
  • 2018: 4.56
  • 2019: 4.67

Pre-2017 average: 7.63
Post-2017 average: 5.06

At first glance, unscheduled cautions appear to fall after stage racing was introduced. However, the decline began prior to 2017.

Regression Analysis

To separate time trends from the policy change, I estimated: 


unscheduled_cautions ~ season_trend + post_2017 + track_type

Results:

  • Significant negative yearly trend (~ -0.57 cautions per year, p < 0.01)
  • No statistically significant post-2017 structural break

These findings remain consistent after:

  • Controlling for track type
  • Adding track fixed effects
  • Clustering standard errors by track

Track-Level Breakdown

The decline in unscheduled cautions occurred within:

  • Short tracks
  • Intermediate tracks
  • Road courses

Superspeedways showed little structural change.

This suggests the result is not driven by schedule composition.

Conclusion

From 2014–2019, unscheduled cautions in the NASCAR Cup Series declined steadily. After accounting for trend and track characteristics, the introduction of stage racing does not produce a detectable increase in unplanned race interruptions.

Stage racing changed the structure of races — but the data do not support the claim that it increased chaos.
